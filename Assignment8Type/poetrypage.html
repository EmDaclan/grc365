Feast of the Fifth Year

Poetry by Chatgpt

Beneath the glow of candle’s gleam, they dine, Five years entwined, like ivy’s climb and twine. A silver stag upon their plates did rest, With wine as red as vows once sworn in jest.

She smiled, “Though time may wrinkle silk and skin,” He laughed, “The fire still crackles deep within.” And so they feasted, love their richest fare, While night slipped past on quiet, velvet air.
